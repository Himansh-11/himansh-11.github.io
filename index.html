üìä Project Overview

Comprehensive analysis of hospital readmission patterns using CMS data

I analyzed 2,496 U.S. hospitals using CMS readmission and HCAHPS data to uncover $207.42M in potential savings. This 3-page Power BI dashboard provides national benchmarking, hospital-level comparisons, and financial improvement strategies.

The analysis combines Hospital Readmissions Reduction Program data with patient satisfaction metrics to identify cost-saving opportunities and performance improvement strategies across the U.S. healthcare system.

The Challenge

Hospital readmissions within 30 days cost Medicare billions annually and indicate gaps in care quality. Despite risk-adjustment models, significant variation exists across hospitals and states. Healthcare administrators lack comprehensive tools to:

  • ‚Üí Identify geographic patterns in readmission performance
  • ‚Üí Compare hospital-level outcomes against national benchmarks
  • ‚Üí Quantify financial impact of excess readmissions
  • ‚Üí Prioritize improvement initiatives based on ROI

üí° Key Findings

Data-driven insights revealing opportunities for improvement

$207.42M Annual Opportunity

Identified potential Medicare savings if all hospitals achieved top-quartile performance (excess ratio < 0.87)

242% Performance Variation

Hospital excess readmission ratios range from 0.48 (52% better than benchmark) to 1.64 (64% worse than benchmark)

Geographic Disparities

State-level performance varies from 0.92 to 1.03, suggesting regional differences in care quality and infrastructure

Weak Satisfaction Correlation

Patient satisfaction shows minimal relationship with readmission rates, indicating distinct quality dimensions

Pneumonia = Highest Cost

Identified the highest-cost condition for targeted intervention programs

56% Above Benchmark

Majority of hospitals exceed national readmission expectations, highlighting systemic improvement opportunities

üéØ Strategic Recommendations

Actionable strategies for reducing readmissions and improving outcomes

  • Targeted Interventions Focus improvement efforts on pneumonia readmissions (the highest cost driver) with evidence-based discharge protocols
  • Best Practice Sharing Implement knowledge transfer programs from top-performing states (ratio < 0.90) to underperforming regions
  • High-Impact Hospitals Prioritize the top 100 hospitals by excess cost for intensive quality improvement initiatives - potential for maximum ROI
  • Condition-Specific Protocols Develop standardized post-acute follow-up procedures for high-readmission conditions
  • State-Level Collaboration Share resources and strategies among hospitals within underperforming states to address systemic issues
